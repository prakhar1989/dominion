Introduction

The unveiling edition of Dominion was organized in January, 2011. It saw encouraging participation from various colleges in the Eastern part of the nation. Even in its debut performance, the event exhibited prodigious success.

Consulting Club, IIM Calcutta, in association with Breakthrough Management Group India, brings to you the second edition of our flagship event. In the forthcoming appearance, the event is set to become much larger: both, in terms of competition, and the accompanying rewards. In addition, the format of the event has been revamped to cover a never-before-explored stage in a typical consulting process.

The event focuses on the pitching stage of a consulting firm's course of work. At the outset, it requires the participating teams to pitch to a prospective (hypothetical) client in the hope of bagging a potential consulting assignment.

Format & Rules

  • Round 1

    • This round will be entirely online
    • Teams will be required to study publically available information on a given listed Indian company with the aim of locating problems noticeable through this secondary research. Problems could be of operational, marketing, financial, or any other functional nature.
    • The submission of the first round will have the following content
      • Two key business problems in the company
      • Reasoning behind choice of problems
      • Analysis of root cause
      • Solutions to the two problems(using only secondary data available)
      • Sources of all data used
    • Submission will have maximum of 6 slides(excluding appendices, title and last cover)
    • Top six teams will qualify for the final round
  • A week before the finals, all six finalist's presentations will be made public. Each team will be expected to thoroughly study the submissions of the other five teams.

  • Round 2 (Final Round)

    • This stage will be conducted in IIM Calcutta campus.
    • Each finalist team will present its case (submission of the first round) in front of the judging panel and the five opponents.
    • Each presentation will be followed by a round of rebuttal where both the judging panel and the opponents question the team in consideration.
Judging Parameters

Round 1:

  • Problem Identification
  • Ramifications of the identified problem | Impact on current state of company | Originality
  • Solution
  • Innovation | Feasibility | Financial Impact | Ease of Implementation | Structure of Presentation

Round 2:

  • Scores of the first round
  • Presentation
  • Delivery | Structure
  • Rebuttal
  • Knowledge of sector | Consistency with solution | Confidence
  • Opponent's Rebuttal
  • Content | Coherence
